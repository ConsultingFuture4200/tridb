### REVIEW  (Liotta — Systems Architecture)
VERDICT: The core bet is right and the discipline is unusually good — TR-1 enforced at review, honest gating, corrections written into the ADRs instead of buried. But strip the varnish and the system has one structural lie and one unproven claim. The lie: the one-WAL thesis does not hold on the vector leg — the HNSW index is a per-backend private RAM cache re-derived O(heap) from the relational store, which multiplies memory by connection count, makes cold start scale with corpus size, and spawned the entire ADR-0014 staleness bug family; DEV-1259 Phase B is not a durability chore, it is the completion of the architecture. The unproven claim: every headline number, including the 4.7 ms flagship, was measured against the v0 heap-backed graph extension — the moat has never been benchmarked as the shipped path (ADR-0013 is the right medicine; take it before publishing another number). On the planner: the SRF-vs-CustomScan agonizing is mostly misdirected energy — the SRF is fine until the surface generalizes; the real planner defect is that the frozen 10% threshold prices a filter-first body that no longer exists (the landed body is a brute-force exact scan whose crossover moves with dimension and corpus size), and a stale-stats misestimate silently re-buys the 171 ms pathology with no runtime escape hatch. The graph AM breaks at 100M edges on arithmetic you can do on a napkin — O(V) vertex lookup makes ingest O(E·V), the metapage counter serializes writers, and the materialize-reachable-set-at-Open predicate is degree^hops. All fixable, all known-shaped. The ONE 10x lever for the quarter: make the HNSW index a genuine WAL-backed, shared-resident access method (ADR-0009 Phase B / DEV-1259) — it simultaneously deletes the rebuild wall, the N-backend RAM multiplication, the cache-invalidation bug class, and the last asterisk on the differentiating FR-7 claim; with the cheap ADR-0013 rewire landed first so the numbers you publish afterward are finally measured on the architecture you are selling.
